Personal Leadership

I wouldn't describe myself as the leader in group projects, but rather as an innovator.
My core values are creativity, positivity, acceptance, teamwork, and ensuring tasks are completed on time.
While I always encourage collaboration, I’m not afraid to push my teammates when necessary to ensure deadlines are met.
I also value feedback and frequently seek second opinions from those around me to ensure that my work aligns with both my vision and the perspectives of others.

Feedback

I ask consistently for feedback from the people around me like friends, group members or in general people in my oil. It's very important for me to know if there is something missing in my work that i happened to forget or if i am not noticing something obvious.
I tend not to ask my teachers for help very often, which is something I’m trying to improve. I want to seek their professional thoughts more frequently and also in that way i help them understand better where I stand in class.
If I compare the number of times I’ve gone to a teacher for feedback from the beginning of the semester until now, I can definitely say I’ve improved. I might ask small, quick questions rather than requesting a full feedback session, but i personally find them more helpful

Target Audience

I was assigned to write the target audience section for the band, so I did some research before starting. I included what the band told us about wanting to attract a younger audience to listen to their music. In the document, I also wrote about where we could help them find this target group. For example, using social media platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube, which are popular with younger people. I also suggested promoting the band through events or collaborations that younger audiences would be interested in, like music festivals or working with influencers in the post-punk and new wave scenes. By combining my research with the band's input, I tried to create a plan that would help them reach the audience they want.

Core values

In one of our classes with Anke, we were tasked with creating an Excel table, splitting it into two sections, and writing each group member’s name. In the first section, we had to list what we believed our own core values were. In the second section, the other group members wrote about us, so like that we could reflect on ourselves. Another time, we had to create an Excel table again, but this time we wrote down our strengths and weaknesses. I listed presenting and distraction as my weaknesses because I don’t enjoy speaking in front of people. However, if I compare myself to the beginning of the semester, I’ve definitely overcome a significant portion of that anxiety. For strengths, I listed "creativity," because I’m always coming up with new ideas, enjoy designing, and have loved creating designs ever since I was little. I also wrote "gets things done on time," because I’m very deadline-oriented and make sure to plan everything I need to do in advance. Additionally, I’m not afraid to speak up and remind my teammates to stay on track when necessary.
