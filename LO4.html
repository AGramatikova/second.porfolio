Portfolio Design

I did a user test on my portfolio with my teacher Jo-An and two other people from my oil.
Jo-An gave me some helpful feedback. She liked my portfolio idea but suggested I make my name and message stand out more instead of the flower design, which made it feel like a flower shop.
So, I decided to simplify the design and blur some elements to keep the focus on my name and message.

The first person that i user tested with, told me that the nav bar was missing something and it could really use a design or some type of interaction.
He couldn’t give me a specific idea in mind, but I came up with a vision that when the nav bar is hovered over it glows.
I tried with multiple colors and thickness but I ended up sticking with a simple white thin glow.

The second person told me that an “about the portfolio” would be very useful. I added it after the “about me” section, I explained what all the element in my website mean to me. I saw it as an introduction to my portfolio.

Project Plan

Making a project plan is from one hand easy but from another it could get confusing.
I find it hard to put my thoughts into words that others should understand when reading.
I do a very deep research about the subject like - what is needed, what isnt so needed and what i could add but is not a must.

Target Audience

I was assigned to write the target audience section for the band, so I did some research before starting.
I included what the band told us about wanting to attract a younger audience(18-25yo) to listen to their music.

In the document, I also wrote about where we could help them find this target group.
For example, using social media platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube, which are popular with younger people.

I also suggested promoting the band through events or collaborations that younger audiences would be interested in, like music festivals or working with influencers in the post-punk and new wave scenes.
By combining my research with the band's input and asking for advice from AI, I tried to create a plan that would help them reach the audience they want.

The MoSCoW table was really helpful for prioritizing tasks. It made it clear what features i absolutely needed and which ones i could skip or save for later. It kept me on track and helped me make better decisions about where to spend time.

Another time, we had to create an Excel table again, but this time we wrote down our strengths and weaknesses.

However, if I compare myself to the beginning of the semester, I’ve definitely overcome a significant portion of that anxiety.
For strengths, I listed "creativity," because I’m always coming up with new ideas, enjoy designing, and have loved creating designs ever since I was little. I also wrote "gets things done on time," because I’m very deadline-oriented and make sure to plan everything I need to do in advance.

Additionally, I’m not afraid to speak up and remind my teammates to stay on track when necessary.

Trello Board

Making a Trello board in group projects is extremly useful, because like that everyone has a clear vision what to do and intill when to do it.
Making one even in an individual project is also helpful, because like that i can keep track of everything i have to do, just like a reminder.
Adding deadlines motivates and makes sure you dont miss something.

In the showed above board i assigned all of the tasks individually to myself and my teammember Miya, avoiding confusion and doing the same thing. Sometimes the assigned tasks have to be changed either to another person or to a further or earlier deadline.
But that is why it is so useful to use trello, because like that the other group members get notified of the changes.

Prototyping

The very first project i had with my group was for how we think 2050 would look like.

Each one of us was brainstorming, i came up with an idea for a drone that could help the workers find damages on the streets easier and sooner, but then we all agreed that the idea is good but will happen way before 2050, so we should think bigger.

After that i came up with a robot that is half robot half human, flys around and whenever he sees any trouble on the streets, such as injuries, street brakage, etc. he would go inspect and help.
We asked the teachers what they thought and Lody told us to go even crazier, and that is exactly what we did.

Kurosh came up with the idea that the robot has body parts that he stores in his stomach, so if there is a big accident that needs urgent care, he would have the supplies to help.

Even when we were done with the project and the presentation time came, i wasn't happy how little we had, so i decided to do a physical prototype of our robot. Asked miya for help and got materials from the oil.
My insparation came from the movie 'The terminator' with Arnold Schwarzenegger
